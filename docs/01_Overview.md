## Executive Summary

**Algebraic Causality Theory (ACT)** is a novel approach to quantum gravity that posits **spacetime as emergent** from fundamental causal relations between quantum events. Unlike traditional approaches that quantize gravity, ACT starts from first principles of causality and quantum mechanics, from which both spacetime geometry and matter fields emerge naturally.

## Core Philosophy

### Foundational Principles

1. **Primacy of Causality**: Causal relations are more fundamental than spacetime
2. **Discreteness at Planck Scale**: Spacetime has a discrete structure at the Planck length
3. **Algebraic Framework**: Physical laws emerge from algebraic relations between events
4. **Emergence Principle**: Continuum physics emerges from discrete causal structure

### Key Innovation

ACT represents a paradigm shift from "quantizing gravity" to **deriving gravity from quantum causality**. This reverses the traditional approach and offers solutions to long-standing problems in theoretical physics.
