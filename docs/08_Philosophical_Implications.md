## 3. Causality and Time

### 3.1 The Primacy of Causality

**Revolutionary claim**: Causality is more fundamental than time.

Traditional physics: Time $t$ as fundamental parameter.
ACT: Causal relations $\prec$ as fundamental, time emerges.

**Temporal becoming vs. Block universe**:
- **Becoming**: Time flows, present is special
- **Block**: All times equally real, no flow

**ACT synthesis**: Block universe at fundamental level, but **perceived flow** emerges from information processing constraints.

### 3.2 The Arrow of Time

**Problem**: Why does time have a direction if fundamental laws are time-symmetric?

**ACT solution**: Entropy gradient from initial conditions + **causal asymmetry**:

$$
\Delta S = k_B \ln \left( \frac{\text{Future cones}}{\text{Past cones}} \right)
$$

The causal structure itself provides the arrow.

### 3.3 Presentism and Eternalism

**Presentism**: Only the present is real.
**Eternalism**: Past, present, future all equally real.

**ACT position**: **Modified eternalism** - all events in $\mathcal{C}$ are real, but "now" is a perspectival concept related to information access.

The causal set $\mathcal{C}$ exists independently, but our $S[\mathcal{C}]$ is human construction.
